<objective>
Close the sale-price write-back discoverability gap with WORDING, not machinery (D-17). A sale is a negotiation with one customer — the price stays on the operation, nothing writes back to the product card (D-15); D-16 rejects sale→card write-back because it would erode criterion 5 via the shared `confirm=1` bypass. The current prefill hints do not say so, implying the price might persist.

Extend BOTH prefill-hint families with the sale-only scope clause — the card-sourced hint AND the batch-sourced hint, because a batch price is equally sale-scoped (`Batch.price_cents` stays frozen), so leaving the batch hint silent implies write-back (D-23). Research verified each string is hard-coded inline at 3 sites (6 total, incl. mobile which CONTEXT.md missed). Extract to two named constants in `app/services/sales.py` mirroring `receipts.py:23`'s `CARD_FILL_HINT`, imported by both route trees (the sanctioned public-constant cross-import at `mobile_sales.py:18`).

Purpose: PROD-07 — the operator understands ДЦ/ПЦ edits made during a sale are saved to that sale only.
Output: two hint constants with the scope clause, six call sites collapsed to them, and tests locking both the wording and the no-write-back guarantee.
</objective>

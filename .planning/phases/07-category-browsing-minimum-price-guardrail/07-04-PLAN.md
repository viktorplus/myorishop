<objective>
Close the one verification gap from 07-VERIFICATION.md (status: gaps_found, 4/5 must-haves) and 07-REVIEW.md's CR-01 Critical finding: `register_sale`'s per-line sale-price parser in `app/services/sales.py` accepts negative values with no guard, unlike `parse_optional_cents` (used for every other money field — cost/sale/catalog/min_sale) which explicitly rejects `< 0`. Because `Product.min_sale_cents` is `None` by design for every product until an operator opts in (D-06), the `below_minimum` check is skipped entirely for the default no-floor state, so a negative price sails through with zero warnings and is written straight into the ledger — undermining the phase's "protected from accidentally underselling" goal for the majority-default case every new product ships into.

Purpose: make the sale-line price parser reject negative amounts the same way every other money field in this codebase already does, closing the data-integrity gap independent of whether a minimum-price floor is configured.
Output: a negative-value guard in `register_sale`'s price parse block, plus regression tests proving a negative `price[]` value is rejected with zero writes both with and without `min_sale_cents` set.
</objective>
